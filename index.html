Topics of Interest

I have recently been diving deeper into several fascinating topics in neuroscience, particularly focusing on how various biological processes and conditions contribute to cognitive decline, stress-related disorders, and neurochemical imbalances. Here are some of the areas I am currently exploring:

  • Synaptic Loss and Cognitive Decline in Alzheimer’s: I recently read a paper on how synaptic loss contributes to cognitive decline in Alzheimer’s patients, with a specific focus on the neuroinflammation caused by microglia and astrocytes releasing cytokines.
  • Cortisol and Stress Hormones in Alzheimer’s Progression: I am interested in studying cortisol, the stress hormone, and how it affects patients with the worst-case progression of Alzheimer's (APOE4 homozygous, amyloid-positive status, and reduced hippocampal volume).
  • Radioimmunoassay Methods and Computational Approaches: I find all three methods of radioimmunoassay fascinating and am keen to create a computational approach to simulate synaptic receptor responses.
  • Serotonin (5-HT) Receptor and Drug Effects: My interest extends to studying the 5-HT receptor (serotonin), particularly examining the effects of different drugs on various neurological conditions.
